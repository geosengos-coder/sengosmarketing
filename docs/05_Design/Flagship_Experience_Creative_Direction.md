# Flagship Experience — Creative Direction

> **Status:** 🟡 Creative direction for approval. No implementation until the direction is chosen. Twenty concepts, scored; a synthesized vision; and a build brief. Governs the [Website Experience Specification](Website_Experience_Specification.md) and the [Business Brain Rendering Architecture](Business_Brain_Rendering_Architecture.md).

## The one idea everything serves

Most SaaS sites *describe* a product. A few *show* it. **Ours will build the product for the visitor, live, using their own business — and then let them talk to it.** The website is not marketing for the OS; the website **is** an instance of the OS, booting a brain for whoever is watching. The unforgettable feeling we're buying: *"It just made me a working AI employee for my company, and I talked to it, in 90 seconds."*

Everything below is judged against that.

## What we refuse (the visitor must never feel these)

Eyebrow labels · stacked sections · repeating cards · content blocks · template grids · fade-in-on-scroll · pricing tables · anything that smells generated. The site is **one continuous world**; what look like "sections" are only **camera states and lighting changes inside a single space.** There are no seams because there's only one room.

## The synthesis — one vision

No single concept is the answer; the flagship is a **braid of six** around one spine:

- **Spine — #5 The Live Onboarding:** the site builds the visitor's own AI, live. Everything leads to this.
- **Visual language — #15 The Living Brain:** the OS is a luminous mind; every node is a *real* business system feeding it; it visibly gets smarter.
- **Opening — #16 The Infinite Zoom:** we begin inside a single 2am phone call and pull back into the intelligence answering it.
- **Emotional through-line — #19 The Night Shift:** the journey runs from dusk to dawn; the payoff is a business that ran itself overnight.
- **Personalization mechanic — #20 Interviews You:** at the turning point it becomes *their* business, not a generic one.
- **Centerpieces — #8 The Conversation + #10 The Blueprint Machine:** a real voice call and a live-generated website, both about *their* company.

**The story in one line:** *We open on the world's problem (the missed call), zoom into the intelligence that solves it, watch it learn a business from its real systems — and then it stops being a story and becomes theirs: they name their company, watch their brain assemble, get their website drafted live, and pick up the phone to actually talk to the AI employee we just built for them, watching it book a real appointment that ripples across a live CRM and calendar. Dawn breaks. Their business is running itself.*

The **"holy sh\*t" moment** is not an effect — it's the realization at the hand-off: *the website made me a working AI employee for my business, and I'm talking to it.*

# Creative Brief — what we will build

## Format

**One continuous vertical journey through a single 3D world**, scroll-scrubbed like a timeline (a camera moving through one space, not a stack of pages). Dusk → night → dawn lighting arc. No section breaks, no eyebrows, no cards, no fade-ins. Transitions are **camera moves, focus pulls, and lighting shifts**. WebGL brain + DOM/Framer-Motion UI composited as one image (per [ADR-0012](../03_Engineering/Decision_Log.md#adr-0012--polyglot-rendering-the-right-renderer-per-responsibility)).

## The journey (beats, not sections)

1. **The Call.** Black. A phone rings. One line of light — a voice waveform. We pull *into* it.
2. **The Mind Wakes.** The waveform resolves into the AI core — dim, dormant, breathing. A single node drifts in and connects: *Website — reading services…* The core brightens a notch.
3. **The Learning.** More real systems arrive and stream data inward — **CRM, Phone, Email, Calendar, QuickBooks, Stripe, Google Reviews, Inventory, Knowledge Base, SMS, Employees.** Each connection fires a live status mote toward the core: *Learning pricing… Understanding appointments… Reading CRM… Training voice… Building knowledge…* An **intelligence level** climbs visibly. The core pulses harder with each. *(No reading required — you watch it get smarter.)*
4. **Activation.** When the last system connects, a **pulse travels the entire network** and the core blooms. The AI is awake. This is the first goosebump.
5. **The Turn — it becomes yours.** The camera settles. One input: *"What's your business called?"* The visitor types. The world **re-skins to their industry** (#20): the nodes relabel to *their* stack, the ambient copy shifts to their world.
6. **The Blueprint (centerpiece #10).** From their name, the AI **drafts their website live** — a wireframe assembling itself in space, section by section, real copy generated for their business. Not a template reveal; a machine thinking on screen.
7. **The Call, Reprised (centerpiece #8 — the real voice demo).** A phone appears. The visitor **actually talks to the AI receptionist we just built for their business.** It answers, converses naturally, and *does things*: it **books an appointment** that drops onto a live calendar beside the brain, **creates a contact** in a live CRM card, and **files a follow-up task** — all animating into the brain as new memories, in real time, while a transcript types out. This is the "holy sh\*t."
8. **Dawn.** The camera pulls back out through the network to reveal it's one of thousands of businesses, each with its own glowing brain — the OS at scale. Light comes up. A single, quiet line and one action: *your AI employee is ready.*

## The Business Brain — redesign directives

The current orb is decorative; it must become **storytelling**:

- **Every node is a real, named system** (Website, CRM, Phone, Email, Calendar, QuickBooks, Stripe, Google Reviews, Inventory, Knowledge Base, Employees, SMS) — labeled, iconographic, legible.
- **Continuous inbound flow:** each system perpetually sends data motes into the core. The core **consumes** them (a visible intake, not ambient drift).
- **The AI reacts:** it pulses on ingestion, breathes at rest, glows brighter as intelligence rises, and **flickers "thought"** when it's working (during the voice call, the core lights along the CRM/Calendar paths as it acts).
- **Live status micro-copy** near active nodes: *Learning pricing… Reading CRM… Training voice…* — short, real, changing.
- **An intelligence state** the visitor can feel rising (a level, a density, a warmth — not a literal progress bar).
- **State, not decoration:** the brain is driven by a real event store, so the marketing brain and the product dashboard are the *same* engine (brand = product).

## The two interactives (must be real, not faked)

- **Live voice call.** The user's directive is explicit: a **real** AI conversation — it answers, speaks, books, updates CRM, creates tasks, shows a transcript. **This revisits [ADR-0011](../03_Engineering/Decision_Log.md#adr-0011--homepage-live-receptionist-simulated-behind-the-real-voiceprovider-seam)** (which chose a simulation for v1). Recommendation: build it **real** on the OpenAI Realtime API behind our `VoiceProvider` seam, but **sandboxed** — a demo business context, hard rate-limits and cost caps per visitor/IP, a short session timer, profanity/abuse guardrails, and an automatic, seamless fallback to a scripted run if the provider is slow or the visitor has no mic. Real when it can be; never broken on stage. *(This is a cost/ops decision to confirm — see Open Decisions.)*
- **Live website blueprint.** Real generation: business name (+ optional industry) → an LLM produces a structured site blueprint (pages, sections, real headlines/copy for *their* business) → we render it assembling as an animated wireframe. Cheap, reliable, and genuinely impressive. Guardrailed and cached.

## Art & motion direction

- **Palette:** the deep-ink stage with the warm luminous brain (brass/bone/sage), evolved to feel **more futuristic and alive** — stronger bloom, real depth, volumetric light, filmic grain, a dusk-to-dawn color arc. Premium, cinematic — never neon/cyberpunk, never stock-AI.
- **Motion is causal, never decorative:** things move because data is flowing, the AI is thinking, or a system just connected. If a motion doesn't mean something, cut it. **No fade-in-on-scroll.**
- **Type:** a licensed display grotesk for the few words we allow ourselves; words are rare and heavy with meaning (the visual carries the story).
- **Sound (optional, opt-in):** a low cinematic bed + subtle ingestion cues; the voice demo is the only required audio. Fully functional muted.
- **One continuous camera:** every beat is the same world under a moving camera and changing light.

## Non-negotiables (accessibility & performance)

- 60fps on modern hardware; graceful degradation and a crafted **reduced-motion / no-WebGL** telling of the same story (real text, static brain). The narrative must land without motion, sound, or a mic.
- The voice and blueprint demos each degrade to a designed fallback; the site is never "broken."
- Mobile gets a first-class, re-choreographed version of the same journey.

## Why this converts

It removes every reason to doubt by **proving** instead of claiming: the visitor sees the AI learn a business, watches it build a website, and *talks to it doing real work* — for their company, before they've signed up. The product sells itself by being used. That is the single greatest sales tool we could build.

## Resolved decisions (founder-approved, 2026-07-12)

1. **Voice — real** ([ADR-0013](../03_Engineering/Decision_Log.md#adr-0013--the-live-voice-agent-is-real-openai--elevenlabs-behind-a-provider-abstraction)): the live call is built real on the **OpenAI Realtime API** with **ElevenLabs** as the default voice, behind the `VoiceProvider` abstraction, with cost caps, session timeouts, abuse protection, and a seamless scripted fallback. Centerpiece — must never feel broken.
2. **Blueprint — truly personalized** ([ADR-0014](../03_Engineering/Decision_Log.md#adr-0014--the-website-blueprint-is-a-real-personalized-analysis)): visitor enters **URL + business name + industry + city**; we **analyze their real website** (strengths, weaknesses, SEO, conversion) and generate a **live animated blueprint** tailored to them.
3. **Scope — full flagship, not an MVP.** Craftsmanship over speed; every interaction judged against "premium, memorable, award-worthy."

## Operational prerequisites (to make the interactives *real*)

The cinematic journey + brain need none of these; the two live centerpieces do:

- **Server-side API keys** (never client, never git): `OPENAI_API_KEY`, `ELEVENLABS_API_KEY` — added by the founder to the deploy secret store / local `.env`.
- **Spend caps** in the OpenAI + ElevenLabs dashboards, plus our own per-visitor/day caps.
- **Voice-gateway host** for the realtime socket (ADR-0001) — confirm Fly.io / Railway / Render.
- Until keys exist, both interactives run their **designed fallbacks**, so the site is always whole.
